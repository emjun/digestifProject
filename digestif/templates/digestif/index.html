digestif

[dee-zhe-steef] (noun)
a tool that helps researchers make their studies more digestible for participants.

Conclusion pages are pages at the end of studies that researchers use to attract visitors, improve their study design and data quality, or simply give back to their participants.

Explore our library of conclusion pages from popular platforms, or create and download your own.

Explore Create

Marketing

Digestif pages that allow participants to share their personalized results lead to snowball recruitment.

Data Quality

The information promised on digestif pages can incentivize participants to contribute more honest data rather than gaming the study.

Feedback

The feedback participants leave as comments or give via email helps researchers identify technical bugs and language that is difficult to understand.

Debrief

Participants are curious about how what they do contributes to science. Researchers have an opportunity to explain the implications of their research on digestif pages.

Ethics

One way researchers can keep participants informed and respect their time and effort is to provide participants with information about the nature of the online study and research.

Outreach

Giving participants additional information on the digestif pages about research aims and findings can be a form of scientific outreach.

The anatomy of a conclusion page

Research Purpose/Study Summary

Explaining the research purpose can educate subjects on your research, and it can be motivating for them to learn what they are contributing to.

Score Interpretation

Score interpretation helps subjects understand their scores, and can be used to explain that scores are not always accurate.

Personalized Results

Personalized results are a way to attract users to your study, and possibly motivate them to do a better job on your experiment in order to get their best score.

Social Comparison

Social comparison is another way of giving results that is often found in conclusion pages.

Other Studies

Conclusion pages are a great place to create traffic for other studies on your platform by providing links to them.

Feedback

Participant feedback is often used to iteratively improve studies as they run.

Additional Resources

Additional resources help educate participants on your field.

Share

Leveraging social media is a common way to create traffic for a study or platform.
